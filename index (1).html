02 / FEATURED PROJECT

Designing Explainable Fuzzy Architectures for Traffic Risk

Final-Year B.Sc Project · May 2026 · Python, scikit-fuzzy
A Mamdani-type fuzzy inference system that scores Indian road-accident risk — and shows exactly why.

Most risk-classification models output a label and stay silent about their reasoning. This project takes the opposite approach: a fuzzy logic engine that ingests six real-world conditions and produces a continuous, human-readable risk score alongside a full trace of which rules fired and why — built for safety auditors, not just data scientists.

  • Designed and implemented a Mamdani-type Explainable Fuzzy Inference System using Python and scikit-fuzzy across six input variables.
  • Built 18 triangular membership functions feeding a 12-rule expert knowledge base, producing continuous risk scores between 0 and 1.
  • Every output carries a full rule-activation trace — making the system auditable, not a black box.
  • Evaluated on a synthetic dataset of 20,000 accident records (4,000 held-out test samples), reaching 84.1% recall on fatal accidents — high sensitivity by design, suited to a safety-screening role.
  • Benchmarked against a Logistic Regression baseline; feature importance analysis identified weather and accident cause as the dominant risk drivers on Indian roads.
